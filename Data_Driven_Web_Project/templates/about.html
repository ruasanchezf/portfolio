About the Data

Data Source

The data is extracted from the Center for Homeland Defense and Security (CDHS). It is part of the Homeland Security Advanced Thinking(HSx) porgram. The research team consisted of two main contributors, David Riedman and Desmond O'Neill. Riedman holds a Masters in Homeland Defense and Security at the Naval Posgraduate School and is a co-founder of the HSx program. O'Neill has a Masters in Forensics Psychology as well as a Masters in Homeland Defense and Security from the Naval Postgraduate School. He is also a co-founder of the HSx program.

Data Information

The data is compiled from many different sources such as: "peer-reviewed studies, government reports, mainstream media, non-profits, private websites, blogs, and crowd-sourced lists." All data has been analzed, filtered, deconflicted, and cross-referenced. This dataset is updated as more data becomes available and is then entered into the existing database.

*This database and its contents have been updated of January 20th, 2020

Terms of use

The data is made up of open source information and is avaliable for download to the general public. The Center for Homeland Defense and Security encourages the use of this data stating that it "is only valuable if it can reach law enforcement leaders, school administrators, policy makers, and reasearchers who can use it to make informed decisions about school safety."
